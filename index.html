ᬳ᭞ Mengenal Lebih Dekat Pulau Dewata: The Island of The Gods

Pulau Dewata memang dikaruniai alam yang mempesona serta budaya yang kaya dan memesona. Saat Anda menjelajahi berbagai keajaiban di sini, Pemerintah Provinsi Bali mengundang Anda untuk mendukung pariwisata lokal dengan menjaga kelestarian alam dan budaya pulau ini.

[en]The Island of Gods is indeed blessed with enchanting nature as well as a rich and fascinating culture. As you explore a myriad of wonders here, Bali Provincial Government invites you to support local tourism by looking after the island’s natural and cultural heritages.

Galeri Wayang Kamasan

Desa Kamasan merupakan induk seni lukis wayang purwa di Bali. Sejarah mencatat, desa Kamasan turut mewarnai perjalanan perkembangan seni lukis Bali. Desa ini bahkan dikenal sebagai “gudang”-nya karya seni lukis wayang klasik, hasil torehan para seniman yang terdiri dari warga kampung itu sendiri.

[en]Nestled in the heart of Bali, Kamasan Village stands as the esteemed birthplace of traditional Balinese wayang painting. Steeped in history, Kamasan has played a pivotal role in shaping the evolution of Balinese painting. Renowned as a "repository" of classical wayang painting masterpieces, the village is home to a community of skilled artists who meticulously craft these intricate works of art.

ᬦ᭞ Aksara Bali: A Legacy of Writing in the Island of the Gods

Aksara Bali, adalah salah satu aksara tradisional Indonesia yang berkembang di Pulau Bali. Aksara ini terutama digunakan untuk menulis bahasa Bali, Sanskerta, dan Kawi, tetapi dalam perkembangannya juga digunakan untuk menulis beberapa bahasa daerah lainnya seperti bahasa Sasak dan Melayu dengan tambahan dan modifikasi.

[en]The Balinese script, also known as Aksara Bali, is one of Indonesia's traditional writing systems that has flourished on the island of Bali. Primarily employed for transcribing the Balinese, Sanskrit, and Kawi languages, its evolution has led to its adoption in writing other regional languages, such as Sasak and Malay, albeit with adaptations and modifications.

Galeri Lontar

Masyarakat di desa ini meyakini lontar memiliki arti penting bagi kehidupan. Salah satunya ialah memotret dan menjadi cermin kehidupan bagi hal-hal yang patut dan tidak patut dilakukan. Dahulu, lontar Bali kuno digunakan sebagai medium komunikasi untuk bersurat kepada kerabat atau bahkan menjadi buku catatan harian. Namun, menulis di atas lontar bukan hal mudah, terlebih yang ditulis bukanlah huruf latin, melainkan aksara Bali, serta menggunakan pangrupak atau alat untuk menulis lontar.

[en]Within the hearts of the Balinese community, lontar holds a profound significance, deeply woven into the fabric of their lives. Serving as a mirror to society, lontar captures the essence of both commendable and reprehensible actions. In the past, ancient Balinese lontar served as a medium of communication, facilitating correspondence with loved ones and even functioning as personal diaries. However, inscribing upon lontar is no easy feat, especially when the script employed is not Latin but rather the intricate Balinese aksara, using the specialized tool known as pangrupak.

Mari Berkunjung ke Museum Pustaka Lontar, Karangasem!

ᬘ᭞ Kecak: A Balinese Dance Drama Depicting Ramayana's Epic Tale

Kecak adalah dramatari seni khas Bali yang lebih utama menceritakan mengenai Ramayana dan dimainkan terutama oleh laki-laki. Tarian ini dipertunjukkan oleh banyak (puluhan atau lebih) penari laki-laki yang duduk berbaris melingkar dan dengan irama tertentu menyerukan "cak" dan mengangkat kedua lengan, menggambarkan kisah Ramayana saat barisan kera membantu Rama melawan Rahwana.

[en]Emerging from the vibrant cultural tapestry of Bali, Kecak stands as a captivating dance drama that retells the grand narrative of the Ramayana. This mesmerizing performance, predominantly performed by male dancers, features a chorus of dozens or even hundreds of men seated in a circular formation. As the rhythm intensifies, they chant "cak" in unison and raise their arms, their synchronized movements bringing to life the Ramayana's epic scenes, particularly the scene where the valiant monkey army aids Rama in his battle against the formidable demon king Ravana.

Galeri Kecak

Tari kecak merupakan ritual shangyang atau tradisi menolak bala yang diselipkan kisah Ramayana di dalamnya. Tari kecak menceritakan tentang pencarian Permaisuri Shinta, Raja Rama dibantu oleh Hanoman. Hanoman lalu memporakporandakan tempat penyekapan Permaisuri Shinta dengan membakarnya. Namun Hanoman justru terkepung oleh prajurit Raja dan Rahwana dan hampir terbakar. Pada awalnya Raja Rama mengalami kekalahan, tetapi tidak menyurutkan kesungguhan Raja Rama menyelamatkan permaisurinya. Raja Rama berdoa dengan sungguh dan kemudian berusaha kembali. Pada akhirnya Raja Rama dapat menyelamatkan Permaisurinya.

[en]Rooted in the spiritual traditions of Bali, Kecak emerges as a captivating ritual dance drama that seamlessly blends the captivating narrative of the Ramayana with the essence of Sanghyang, an ancient ritual aimed at warding off evil spirits. The performance unfolds as a mesmerizing spectacle, recounting the valiant tale of Rama's relentless pursuit to rescue his beloved Queen Sita from the clutches of the demon king Ravana. In this captivating dance drama, Rama seeks the assistance of Hanuman, the mighty monkey warrior, to embark on a perilous quest to locate the abducted Sita. Hanuman, with his exceptional strength and unwavering loyalty, successfully infiltrates Ravana's kingdom, locating Sita and setting fire to the palace in an attempt to free her. However, Hanuman finds himself surrounded and besieged by Ravana's army, narrowly escaping capture and the fiery inferno. Undeterred by the initial setback, Rama, fueled by unwavering determination and love for his queen, fervently prays for guidance and renewed strength. With renewed vigor, Rama rallies his forces and launches a fierce assault on Ravana's kingdom, ultimately emerging victorious in the epic battle. In a triumphant climax, Rama is reunited with his beloved Sita, their bond stronger than ever, a testament to the power of love and perseverance.

Mari Berkunjung ke Pura Luhur Uluwatu, Badung!

ᬭ᭞​​ Ubud: A Captivating Blend of Nature's Beauty and Flourishing Arts

Ubud terutama terkenal diantara para wisatawan mancanegara karena terletak di antara sawah dan hutan yang berjurang-jurang yang membuat pemandangan alam sangat indah. Selain itu, Ubud dikenal karena seni dan budaya yang berkembang sangat pesat dan maju. Denyut nadi kehidupan masyarakat Ubud tidak bisa dilepaskan dari kesenian. Di sini banyak pula terdapat galeri-galeri seni, serta arena pertunjukan musik dan tari yang digelar setiap malam secara bergantian di segala penjuru desa.

[en]Ubud's allure for international tourists stems primarily from its captivating location. Nestled amidst emerald rice terraces and dramatic, ravine-like forests, Ubud boasts breathtaking natural beauty. However, Ubud's charm extends far beyond its picturesque landscapes. This vibrant town flourishes with a rich tapestry of art and culture, deeply ingrained in the very pulse of its community. Art enthusiasts flock to Ubud's numerous art galleries, each showcasing a treasure trove of artistic expression. As night falls, the village comes alive with a vibrant array of music and dance performances, staged in various locations throughout Ubud. Every evening, a different corner of the village transforms into a stage, offering visitors a captivating glimpse into the heart and soul of Balinese artistic traditions.

Galeri Ubud

Sudah sejak tahun 1920-an, Ubud terkenal di antara wisatawan barat. Kala itu pelukis Jerman; Walter Spies dan pelukis Belanda; Rudolf Bonnet menetap di sana. Mereka dibantu oleh Tjokorda Gde Agung Sukawati, dari Puri Agung Ubud. Sekarang karya mereka bisa dilihat di Museum Puri Lukisan, Ubud.

[en]As early as the 1920s, Ubud began to captivate the hearts of Western travelers, establishing itself as a haven for artists. German painter Walter Spies and Dutch painter Rudolf Bonnet found their creative haven in this enchanting town, inspired by its serene beauty and rich cultural heritage. With the support of Tjokorda Gde Agung Sukawati, the esteemed ruler of Puri Agung Ubud, these visionary artists embarked on a remarkable journey, leaving an indelible mark on Ubud's artistic landscape. Today, art enthusiasts can immerse themselves in the legacy of these pioneering artists by visiting the Museum Puri Lukisan, Ubud. This treasure trove of artistic expression houses an impressive collection of works by Spies, Bonnet, and their contemporaries, offering a glimpse into the artistic renaissance that flourished in Ubud during the early 20th century.

ᬓ᭞​​ Tenganan Pegringsingan: The Remnant of Ancient Bali

Desa Tenganan merupakan salah satu desa dari tiga desa Bali Aga, selain Trunyan dan Sembiran. Bali Aga adalah desa yang masih mempertahankan pola hidup yang tata masyarakatnya mengacu pada aturan tradisional adat desa yang diwariskan nenek moyang mereka. Bentuk dan besar bangunan serta pekarangan, pengaturan letak bangunan, hingga letak pura dibuat dengan mengikuti aturan adat yang secara turun-temurun dipertahankan.

[en]Nestled amidst the lush landscapes of East Bali, the village of Tenganan Pegringsingan stands as a testament to the enduring traditions of the Bali Aga people. As one of the three remaining Bali Aga villages, alongside Trunyan and Sembiran, Tenganan Pegringsingan offers a glimpse into a way of life deeply rooted in ancestral customs and reverence for nature. Stepping into Tenganan Pegringsingan is akin to embarking on a journey through time. The village's layout, architecture, and daily rituals bear witness to the unwavering commitment of its inhabitants to preserving their rich cultural heritage. From the meticulously designed houses and courtyards to the sacred placement of temples, every aspect of Tenganan Pegringsingan reflects the enduring wisdom and harmony that have guided the Bali Aga for generations.

Galeri Tenganan Pegringsingan

Berasal dari Desa Tenganan Pegringsingan, Bali, Geringsing menjadi bukti nyata keuletan seni dan warisan budaya masyarakat Bali Aga. Kain tenun ikat ganda yang luar biasa ini, dibuat dengan cermat selama dua hingga lima tahun, merupakan puncak dari keahlian pengrajin Bali. Pola rumit yang menghiasi kain Geringsing bukan hanya keajaiban estetika; mereka memiliki makna simbolis yang mendalam. Nama "Geringsing" sendiri berasal dari kata Bali "gring" yang berarti "sakit" dan "sing" yang berarti "tidak", mengacu pada keyakinan bahwa kain suci ini memiliki kekuatan pelindung, menangkal penyakit dan kemalangan. Selama berabad-abad, Geringsing telah memainkan peran penting dalam kehidupan spiritual dan budaya masyarakat Bali Aga. Kain ini menghiasi tubuh individu selama ritual sakral, seperti upacara potong gigi dan pernikahan, berfungsi sebagai penghubung nyata dengan tradisi leluhur dan kepercayaan spiritual.

[en]Emerging from the heart of Tenganan Pegringsingan, Bali, Geringsing stands as a testament to the enduring artistry and cultural heritage of the Bali Aga people. This exceptional double ikat woven textile, meticulously crafted over a span of two to five years, represents a pinnacle of Balinese craftsmanship. The intricate patterns adorning Geringsing fabrics are not merely aesthetic marvels; they embody profound symbolic significance. The name "Geringsing" itself derives from the Balinese words "gring" meaning "sick" and "sing" meaning "not," alluding to the belief that this sacred cloth possesses protective powers, warding off illness and misfortune. For centuries, Geringsing has played a pivotal role in the spiritual and cultural life of the Bali Aga community. It adorns the bodies of individuals during sacred rites of passage, such as tooth-filing ceremonies and weddings, serving as a tangible link to ancestral traditions and spiritual beliefs.
